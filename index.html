About

I am an undergraduate at Princeton University, studying Computer Science with a minor in Statistics and Machine Learning. My research interests include explainable AI, algorithmic fairness, human-computer interaction, representation learning, and computational social science.

I currently conduct research on AI explainability and fairness in the Ramaswamy Lab, advised by Dr. Vikram Ramaswamy and Dr. Pedro Paredes. For the most current overview of my research, please see my CV.

Research

  • Network Concept Pruning. Concept-based pruning methods for convolutional neural networks, with applications to interpretability, robustness, and reducing reliance on spurious features.
  • ProxyFinder. Computational social science methods for selecting proxy variables for missing survey constructs, with emphasis on prediction, validity, and fairness considerations.
  • LLM-mediated collaboration. Human-computer interaction research on using language models to distribute procedural instructions across team members during collaborative tasks.
